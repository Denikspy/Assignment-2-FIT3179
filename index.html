How are the populous nations doing in the COVID-19 vaccination race?

COVID-19 vaccination rates across the world

Here we can see a map outlining the vaccination rates across the world per 10,000 population.

The results are interesting. For example, we can see that Greenland have nearly the same figure as China per 10,000.

One has a population of 56,367.. and the other a population just under 1.5 billion!



Top 10 Vaxxed Countries

It is evident from the bar chart that China is clearly leading the vaccination race, having more then double total vaccines then the next highest in India.

Simalarly, India and the USA also rank in the top 3. All these countires have immense populations.

However, it is important to ask if these numbers are high because of their population, or wether they are genuinely advanced in terms of vaccinating their nation.



Top 10 Vaxxed Countries (Normalised)

Now taking a look at the same chart, but this time normliased, we can see that China does not even rank in the top 10

In fact, none of the countires that in the top 10 of vaccination numbers rank in the top 10 of normalised vaccination numbers.

So we have a rough idea from these two graphs that countries with large populations aren't necessarily more effective at vaccinating. Is there a way to see definitively if there is a relationship?



Population vs Vaccine Scatterplot: Is there a relationship?

We have adjusted vaccines on this scatterplot to be normlalised per 10,000 population, hence allowing us to see if there is any relationship between thw two.

We can clearly see that a countries population does not mean they are better at vaccinating their nation.

Rather, this would be effected by other factors.
